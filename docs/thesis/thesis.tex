\begin{abstract}
    As households start producing electricity using renewable energy, electricity markets are left vulnerable to local demand shocks. In this paper, by means of a theoretical model, I show how demand shocks can lead to persistent imbalances in prices in cross-border markets. This results invalidates the idea that further material integration of regional electricity markets is sufficient to shield consumers from electricity price volatility.
\end{abstract}
